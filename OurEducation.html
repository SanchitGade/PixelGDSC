Education

The right to education means every child can go to school and learn new things, helping them grow smart, make friends, and dream big. It ensures that learning is for everyone, no matter who you are or where you live.

Health

The right to health means that every person has the right to access healthcare services, medicines, and information to stay healthy and well. It ensures that everyone, including children, can receive the care and support they need to live a happy and healthy life, free from illness and disease.

Protection

The right to protection ensures that children and individuals are safe from harm, abuse, and discrimination, providing them with security and support to live freely and without fear.

Discrimination

The right against discrimination means that everyone should be treated fairly and equally, no matter who they are or where they come from. It's about respecting each other and making sure nobody is treated unfairly because of things like their race, religion, or background.

Express

The right to express means every child can speak their thoughts, feelings, and ideas freely, knowing they'll be listened to and respected. It lets children share their voice, be heard, and make a difference in the world.s
